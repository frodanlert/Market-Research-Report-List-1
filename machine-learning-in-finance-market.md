<p><p>Machine learning in finance involves various market types, each with distinct methodologies. Supervised learning utilizes labeled data to predict outcomes, such as stock prices. Unsupervised learning identifies patterns or groupings in unlabeled data, useful for clustering similar assets. Semi-supervised learning combines both approaches, leveraging a small amount of labeled data with vast unlabeled data to enhance predictions. Reinforcement learning focuses on decision-making through trial and error, optimizing trading strategies by learning from past actions and their outcomes.</p></p>
