<p><p>The adult lower limb orthoses market is primarily divided into dynamic and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to facilitate movement, providing support while allowing flexibility, which aids in rehabilitation and enhances mobility. In contrast, static orthotics are rigid and offer support without allowing joint movement, primarily used for immobilization or correction of deformities. Both types play crucial roles in managing conditions such as neurological disorders, musculoskeletal injuries, and post-surgical recovery, catering to different patient needs and rehabilitation goals.</p></p>
