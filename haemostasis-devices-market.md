Vascular Closure Devices (VCD) are specifically engineered to seal vascular access sites, providing rapid hemostasis while minimizing complications. They enhance patient recovery and reduce bleeding risk. Compression devices, on the other hand, apply pressure to control bleeding, often used in procedures involving larger arterial access points. Both types of devices play crucial roles in ensuring patient safety and improving surgical outcomes.</p></p>
